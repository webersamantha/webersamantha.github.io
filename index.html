About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Psychiatric University Hospital Zurich, working at the intersection of psychiatry, artificial intelligence, and digital health.

My academic path started in Health Science and Technology, where I became fascinated by the brain and clinical neuroscience, particularly psychiatry. During my PhD, I worked with functional neuroimaging in psychosomatic research, trying to better understand the neural mechanisms underlying mental health. While this work was scientifically exciting, I increasingly felt that I wanted my research to have a more direct impact on patients.

The emergence of modern AI systems and large language models opened up a new path. Digital tools make it possible to reach people who might otherwise never receive help, and psychiatry is an area where accessible, scalable support can make a real difference. Since then, my work has focused on developing and evaluating AI-based systems, such as conversational agents and structured clinical assessment tools, that can support mental health care in a safe, ethical, and clinically meaningful way.

More broadly, I am interested in how AI can be responsibly integrated into healthcare to support clinicians and improve access to care, while preserving the human aspects that are central to psychiatry.

Outside of research, I spend a lot of time staying active. I enjoy weightlifting and running, love good food, and share my home with two very opinionated cats.
